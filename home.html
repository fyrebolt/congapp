Why should I vote?

  • Civic Duty: Voting encourages individuals to actively participate in the democratic process.
  • Selection of Representatives: By voting, you can choose representatives who resonate with your values and beliefs, ensuring your voice is accurately represented in government.
  • Community Engagement: Voting fosters community engagement by necessitating awareness of current events and active participation in public discourse, encouraging informed conversations and an engaged citizenry.
  • Equality and Inclusivity: Voting promotes equality and inclusivity, providing every eligible citizen, regardless of their background, with an equal voice in politics.
  • Advocating Change: You can advocate change and progress in society by voting, as it allows you to express your preferences and work toward the evolution and reform of various aspects of the nation!

Does my vote event matter?

Many people believe that with so many other Americans voting, their single vote won't matter. But many elections, especially local elections, often end in thin margins, making individual voters so important. If you give up your vote, you allow others who might not have your best interests at heart to make decisions about your community!
